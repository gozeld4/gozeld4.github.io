Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) are persistent environmental contaminants linked to serious health effects, yet rapid and affordable field detection remains a significant technical challenge. This project develops a resistivity-based lateral flow sensor that leverages the unique fluorophilic affinity of fluorous polyaniline (F-PANI) to selectively concentrate and detect PFAS compounds at trace concentrations.

The sensor design exploits the conductivity changes in polyaniline upon PFAS binding, enabling label-free, instrument-free detection via simple resistance measurement. Sensor fabrication, material characterization (SEM, FTIR, cyclic voltammetry), and performance testing across a panel of common PFAS compounds are reported. Selectivity against common water matrix interferences is demonstrated.

This work contributes a practical, scalable approach to environmental monitoring that could enable widespread community-level PFAS surveillance in drinking water sources.
