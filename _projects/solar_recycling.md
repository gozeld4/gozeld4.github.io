As solar energy adoption accelerates across New England, the end-of-life management of photovoltaic panels is emerging as a significant environmental challenge. Solar panel waste contains hazardous materials including lead and cadmium, making proper recycling critical. This project develops an optimization model to determine the ideal geographic placement of recycling facilities across Massachusetts, balancing transport costs, population density, and environmental equity.

The approach combines geospatial clustering, facility location optimization (p-median and coverage models), and sociodemographic weighting to ensure equitable access. Panel waste volume projections are incorporated using state-level solar installation data, allowing the model to plan infrastructure for both current and projected future needs.

Results identify facility configurations that minimize total transport distance while maintaining coverage requirements, offering practical recommendations to policymakers and waste management planners in the Commonwealth.
