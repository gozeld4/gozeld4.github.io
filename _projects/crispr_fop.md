<div class="project-section-card">
<h4>The Problem</h4>
<p>Fibrodysplasia Ossificans Progressiva (FOP) is an ultra-rare genetic disorder in which soft tissues — ligaments, skeletal muscles — are progressively converted into bone through heterotopic ossification. The root cause is a single nucleotide substitution (R206H) in the <em>ACVR1</em> gene that locks the receptor in a constitutively active state, driving aberrant bone growth, joint fusion, and severe mobility loss. Life expectancy is approximately 40 years, and no cure currently exists — making precision gene editing of muscle stem cells one of the most compelling therapeutic approaches available.</p>
</div>

<div class="project-section-card">
<h4>The Approach</h4>
<p>The proposed therapeutic uses a muscle-targeted CRISPR-Cas9 ribonucleoprotein (RNP) complex encapsulated in lipid nanoparticles (LNPs). Delivering Cas9 and the sgRNA as a pre-assembled RNP ensures immediate but transient editing activity, reducing risks of long-term genome instability and off-target mutations compared to viral delivery. LNP synthesis employs a microfluidic flow-focusing mixer with square channel geometry for precise control over particle size and uniformity. The downstream manufacturing process integrates Tangential Flow Filtration (TFF) for concentration, dialysis for buffer exchange, and 0.22 μm sterile filtration — designed to meet clinical-grade quality standards at scale.</p>
</div>

<div class="project-section-card">
<h4>Results</h4>
<p>Based on global epidemiological data and estimated underdiagnosis rates, the model projects a worldwide FOP prevalence of ~10,839 individuals, requiring 80,064 mg of drug product annually. Engineering analysis of the microfluidic system yielded an optimized mixing time of 17.1 ms and a minimum channel length of 0.285 cm — both within a mechanically stable operating range. The proposed manufacturing configuration runs 50 microfluidic chips in parallel for 2,000 hours per year to meet annual output targets. The integrated strategy — spanning nanoparticle formulation, muscle-targeting ligands, and scalable manufacturing — establishes a feasible translational pathway toward clinical development.</p>
</div>
