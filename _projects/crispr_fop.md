Fibrodysplasia ossificans progressiva (FOP) is a rare and devastating genetic disorder caused by a gain-of-function mutation in the ACVR1 gene, leading to progressive heterotopic ossification of soft tissues. No approved disease-modifying treatments currently exist. This project designs and evaluates lipid nanoparticle (LNP) formulations for the systemic delivery of CRISPR-Cas9 ribonucleoprotein complexes targeting the ACVR1 R206H mutation.

Formulation parameters — including lipid composition, N/P ratio, PEGylation density, and particle size — are systematically optimized to maximize encapsulation efficiency, endosomal escape, and in vivo biodistribution to target musculoskeletal tissues. Off-target editing risk and immunogenicity are assessed using predictive computational tools and published in vitro benchmarks.

This project contributes to the growing body of work demonstrating the feasibility of LNP-based CRISPR delivery for rare monogenic diseases, with a clear translational pathway toward IND-enabling studies.
