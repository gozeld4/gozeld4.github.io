Methane is a potent greenhouse gas, and dilute methane emissions from coal mine ventilation air represent a significant and largely unmitigated source of global warming. This project investigates copper-doped natural zeolites as low-cost catalytic materials for the oxidative destruction of sub-1% methane concentrations — a regime where conventional combustion technologies are ineffective.

Natural zeolites were ion-exchanged with copper at varying Cu loadings and characterized by ICP-OES, XRD, BET surface area analysis, and temperature-programmed reduction (TPR). Catalytic activity for methane oxidation was evaluated in a fixed-bed reactor across a range of temperatures, flow rates, and feed compositions. Cu-exchanged clinoptilolite demonstrated promising light-off temperatures and stability, outperforming synthetic zeolite benchmarks on a cost-per-performance basis.

A patent application is currently pending based on the novel catalyst formulations identified in this work, reflecting its potential for direct industrial deployment in mine ventilation air methane (VAM) mitigation.
