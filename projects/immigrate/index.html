Immigrate: A Choice-Based Interactive Narrative System

A choice-based interactive narrative game that simulates the immigration experience through branching, consequence-driven storytelling.

Developed as a project for MIT’s Comparative Media Studies program, Immigrate is a choice-based narrative game designed to place players inside the lived experience of immigration — navigating bureaucratic systems, cultural displacement, linguistic barriers, and moments of unexpected connection. The project explores how interactive media can build empathy in ways that static storytelling cannot, by making the player an active participant in decisions that carry real narrative weight.

The system is built on a branching story engine where choices cascade across multiple arcs, and no playthrough resolves identically. Dialogue trees were constructed to reflect the emotional and logistical complexity of the immigration process, drawing on documented first-person accounts and policy research. The narrative intentionally avoids a single “correct” path — mirroring the reality that outcomes in immigration are shaped as much by circumstance as by choice.

The CMS class context pushed the project beyond technical execution toward a deeper engagement with medium and message: how interactivity changes what a story can say, and who it can reach.
